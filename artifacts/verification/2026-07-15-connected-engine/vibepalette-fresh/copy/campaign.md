## LinkedIn

A useful palette should reflect the page—not whichever tiny pixel happens to be loudest.

VibePalette is a Chrome and Edge extension that captures the current tab and extracts an export-ready palette. Its documented approach combines dominance-aware RGB bins with spatial sampling and balanced selection to preserve meaningful color diversity.

Choose 5–10 colors, work in HEX, RGB, or HSL, then copy the palette or export it as PNG, CSS custom properties, JSON, or a Coolors link.

The project is built with Vanilla JavaScript, tested with real-image regression fixtures, and released under the MIT license. Explore the source repository on GitHub.

### First comment

I built VibePalette around a simple workflow: capture the page you are already viewing, extract its meaningful colors, and move those colors into the next stage of your work.

The extraction approach combines quantized RGB bins, global dominance scoring, cell winners, corner averages, and top-band sampling. The goal is to keep small saturated details from taking over while preserving color diversity that actually belongs to the image.

The result can be copied or exported as PNG, CSS custom properties, JSON, or a Coolors link. The source is MIT-licensed and available on GitHub. I would especially value feedback on the palettes produced from the real pages you use every day.
